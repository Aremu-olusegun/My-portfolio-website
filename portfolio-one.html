Nigerian Railway
E-Ticketing
App.

Project

NRC(Nigerian Railway Corperation) is a Nigerian Railway sample mobile E-ticketing application which allows train travellers to books unreserved tickets of suburban railways using their smartphone.
This was a one man project, while this UI design could be further improved with better research, more possibilities could have been explored.


The idea and introduction of the App came out of necessity, i was frustrated after making a frantic search on the internet looking for solutions for an NRC e-ticketing for smartphone users. This project was also thought through from a business perspective, while train services are needed to ease Nigeria transport system, the smartphone population should be the country's target proposition.

Identifying pain-points

Below were the pain points i noted when following the above scenario.


Transaction

I spoke to three different users of the train service. I was told they are required to visit the physical location before a transaction is made.

Existing solution

A government web app that appears to provide reserved ticketing, while the UI is debatable, smartphone users will have preferred a more suitable solution.

There is a smartphone revolution happening in Nigeria.

With such a fast-paced life, people don't have the time to stand in queues for tickets, neither do they have time to spend more than a minute to book a ticket on an App.

Problem statement

1. Non-existent smartphone app.

2. Passengers cant book train rides except they visit the train station.

3. Revenue is limited to offline sales.

Goals

Shedding light on the issues mentioned above, a redesign of the entire in-app experience. was required.
Three main goals were defined:

1. Create a new UI design, ensure user flow between the core functions of the application like ticket booking and payment interface.

2. Implement additional features that allow innovation, ease the user experience and boost the sales from tickets.

3. Design a completely new visual apperance of the interface to keep it at par with the modern day smartphone application.

Research

Firstly, to get a rough representation of this potential new user, i created a proto-persona.

Proto-Persona

I spoke to three friends, two of whom were students who use pubic transport on a regular basis. This helped me create the below proto persona and step in to the shoes of the potential user of the app, who relies on the train to travel places.

Wireframes

I digitized the sketches and created the lo-fi wireframe to define the visual form on the screen, presentation and arrangements of all the elements. From this, we can define the visual and create the high fidelity wireframes.

Understanding business needs

I could not just design it according to a fraustrated user, i had to keep in mind, the business needs and the constraints that were laid forward. While enough data could not be gathered from official source. Commuters will always want ease in transportation, avoid ticket queue and travel safely.

High-fidelity

Key Takeaways

This project has provided a learning experience like none other. Our designing skills are major players when defining the way a product solves a problem or create a better user experience. Usually our clients provide us with a brief, but to work without one, allowed me to create an enhanced visual solution that the users needed.

A design especially a mobile App, is a never ending process . It is constantly evolving and getting better as we understand our users better. Here is when usability testing is a treasure chest.

Along with that, the future scope of the ticketing experience is beyond what the current system allows.

Thank you for reading till to this point. Now we wait for an official app
