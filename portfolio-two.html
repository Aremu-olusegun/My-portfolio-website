Nuno brand drones
(product - Autel Evo)
Website.

Project

Autel Robotics is a US-based company providing aerial robotics to the public sector, public safety, enterprise, and commercial operations. While Autel robotics is on the list of best selling drone company, this drone mode was released for direct competition with other drones in its specs category. From newbie drone users to FAA part 107 licensed pilot, this is a solid effort from Autel robotics.

Creating the Nuno brand was thought through from the perspective of creating a new brand that has a winning product, with the sole aim of creating brand recognition within the drone community.

Reporting for work

Like the internet and GPS before them, drones are evolving beyond their military origin to become powerful business tools. They’ve already made the leap to the consumer market, and now they're being put to work in commercial and civil government applications from firefighting to farming. That’s creating a market opportunity that's too large to ignore.

Problem statement

1. Barriers to entry is a nightmare for brands trying to enter a fiercely contested industry, incumbents who own the large market share create different barriers to entry for new names, the need to convince users will be a herculean task but still not impossible.

2. Drone users are either hobbyists or professional/business users, for business users drones that provide the best option are pricey. Drone buyers are not concerned about paying more, except usability and product quality.
Established brands control the market, from data gathered.

Goals

Create a new brand and website/landing page that can contest with incumbents by showing superiority in product features.

Low-fidelity Wireframes

Business Goals

1. Create a brand that is not limited to consumer use.

2. To become the go to brand for commercial usage and also hobbyists.

3. To dispel negative bias towards viable drone use.

4. As with other emerging technological markets, the engagement model for drones is somewhat complex and in transition, the business plans to navigate this.

The takeaways

I have had to develop a growing interest with drone technology, this was a project that broadened my knowledge as regards this interest. Drones have muliple use cases, thus, this project was carefully selected, industries ranging from insurance, agriculture, construction demonstrates the broad range in commercial drone applications. From getting to hard to reach locations, inspecting construction sites for safety, this has helped increase work efficiency. Flight, speed, dramatic photography - it's easy to see why drones appeal to consumers.
